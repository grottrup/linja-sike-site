Introduction to arcsinc

The arcsinc function is the inverse of the (unnormalized) sinc function, or sin(x)/x

In other words, y=arcsin(x) if sin(y)/y=x

Motivation

The arcsinc function was necessary in providing a solution to the Arctan and Newton's Method problem

Properties

arcsinc(x) has a domain of [-0.217233628211222, 1]
and a range of [0, 4.493409457909064]

The derivative of arcsinc(x) is:

    arcsinc(x)

-----------------

cos(arcsinc(x))-x

and its integral is:

x*arcsinc(x)-Si(arcsinc(x))

where Si(x) is the sine integral function

y=arcsinc(x) satisfies the differential equation:

        y

y' = --------

     cos(y)-x
