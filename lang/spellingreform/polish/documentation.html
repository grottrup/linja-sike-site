  1. All of these "example" sounds are for my dialect of english specifically, especially for the vowels. If you pronounce some words differently, then you might spell things differently.
  2. My /t/ phoneme has a wide variety of different realizations, which I reflect in writing. If these are different for you, you might spell things differently.
  3. All "ts" clusters are spelled with the letter c, even if they span multiple morphemes (like with plural endings). For example, the plural of tekst is teksc
  4. My dialect does not distinguish between these vowels. If distinguishing them is important to you, you may come up with an orthographic solution.
  5. The letter i is used for the /i/ sound everywhere except for as the unstressed last sound of the word, where it is spelled y. For example: scene, see, decree, and wacky are spelled sin, si, dekri and łaky. Additionally, i is used instead of y before nk/ng, like in "sing."
  6. These vowels are actually distinct in my dialect, but I chose not to distinguish them in writing.
  7. Yes, this is how I pronounce the word "strut." Cry about it.
